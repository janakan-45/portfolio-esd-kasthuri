Industry Attitudes and Expectations – HR Aspect

Mr. Mahinthan Subenthiran , Group Manager – Human Resources, NU, MT, MW (SL)

Introduction

This session focused on understanding the role of Human Resources (HR) in organizations, the expectations HR has from employees, and how students/professionals can align their skills with industry needs for career growth.

Understanding key HR concepts and definitions
Interactive learning session with HR professional
Industry Attitudes and Expectations - HR Aspect session

Key Takeaways

  • HR is not just a job but a practice focusing on managing people, culture, and compliance.
  • Core HR functions include recruitment, onboarding, employee relations, learning & development, performance management, and HR analytics.
  • HR evaluates both technical and soft skills during interviews, emphasizing cultural fit, attitude, and enthusiasm.
  • Employees should know about contracts, policies, rights, and labor laws before joining.
  • Growth depends on skill development, adaptability, and leadership qualities—not just tenure.
  • Language proficiency (especially English), presentation skills, and public speaking are crucial for career success.
  • Continuous learning and stepping out of the comfort zone enable career progression.
  • Current trends: AI, automation, remote work, and diversity/inclusion initiatives.

What I Learned

Goal Alignment

The importance of aligning personal goals and values with organizational objectives.

Professionalism

How professionalism, punctuality, and networking platforms (like LinkedIn) impact career opportunities.

Company Policies

The need to understand company policies, reporting lines, and job descriptions clearly.

Adaptability

Why adaptability, critical thinking, and digital proficiency matter more than ever in today's HR-driven workplace.

Personal Reflection

"This session gave me a clearer picture of HR's strategic role in shaping an organization and the workforce. I realized that success is built on continuous learning, stepping beyond my comfort zone, and demonstrating both technical and interpersonal skills. Week 9

Managing skills &building work-life balance

Kugashini Gunapagan (MHPSS practitioner & counsellor)

Introduction

This session conducted by Kugashini Gunapagan (MHPSS practitioner & counsellor) focused on stress management techniques and strategies to achieve a healthier work-life balance. It highlighted practical exercises, the psychology of stress, and the importance of cultivating habits that enhance resilience, well-being, and productivity.

Key Takeaways

  • Stress can be acute, episodic, or chronic — understanding the type helps in applying the right coping strategies.
  • Common causes of work stress include heavy workload, lack of boundaries, role conflicts, and poor time management.
  • Managing stress involves lifestyle habits (sleep, exercise, social connection) and professional support when necessary.
  • Work-life balance contributes directly to productivity, mental health, and overall life satisfaction.
  • Simple daily practices such as gratitude, breathing exercises, and self-compassion can significantly reduce stress levels.

What Was Learned

Stress Awareness

Recognizing the difference between acute, episodic, and chronic stress helps in applying the right coping strategies.

Work-Life Balance

Balancing personal well-being with professional responsibilities improves productivity, mental health, and relationships.

Practical Coping Strategies

Using tools like breathing exercises, gratitude practice, digital detox, and time management charts to reduce stress effectively.

Resilient Mindset

Building boundaries, learning to say “no,” and applying small positive changes weekly to create long-term resilience.

Personal Reflection

"This session helped me realize that stress management is not about eliminating stress but learning to respond to it with healthier strategies. I am motivated to practice gratitude, adopt better time management methods, and maintain a stronger balance between work and personal life. By applying these techniques consistently, I can reduce stress, enhance my resilience, and improve both productivity and well-being."
